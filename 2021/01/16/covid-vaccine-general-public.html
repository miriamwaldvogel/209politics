Hundreds of COVID Vaccines Released to General Public

January 16, 2021 at 9:54 pm

At least several hundred COVID-19 vaccine doses were released to the general public in Stockton beginning Saturday morning. Vaccinations took place at the parking lot of the San Joaquin County Agricultural Commissioner at 2101 East Earhart Avenue, and are set to continue on Sunday. People in cars lined up on Arch Airport Road to get shots, forming a queue of about three-quarters of a mile and almost three hours. The doses appear to be from Moderna, according to more than a dozen people who received a dose on Saturday.

It is unclear why the vaccines were made available. Like most California counties, San Joaquin is still in Phase 1A of its vaccine distribution, where only healthcare workers and long-term care residents are eligible for shots. When asked if doses were reserved for that group, a worker at the site stated that they were not turning anyone away, according to one person who was vaccinated on Saturday.

The state has said that shots may be offered to people in lower priority groups for three reasons: if the vaccination demand subsides in the current tier, if doses are about to expire, or if doses have already been thawed and would otherwise be wasted. Roughly 1 million vaccinations have already been administered.

A receptionist for San Joaquin County Public Health Services could not confirm the story.
