H.R. 40

Bill - To address the fundamental injustice, cruelty, brutality, and inhumanity of slavery in the United States and the 13 American colonies between 1619 and 1865 and to establish a commission to study and consider a national apology and proposal for reparations for the institution of slavery, its subsequent de jure and de facto racial and economic discrimination against African Americans, and the impact of these forces on living African Americans, to make recommendations to the Congress on appropriate remedies, and for other purposes.

H.R. 40 would establish a commission to study the history of slavery in the United States and the resulting systemic racism, and recommend reparations (or “appropriate remedies'') to Congress. Support for reparations had gradually grown over the years, particularly after Ta-Nehisi Coates’ article in The Atlantic titled The Case for Reparations. H.R. 40 was first proposed in 1989 by Rep. John Conyers (D-MI), who then reintroduced it every year until he left Congress in 2017.
