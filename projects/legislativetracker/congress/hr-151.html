H.R. 151 is also known as the Preventing Future Pandemics Act. The bill would direct the State Department to work with international partners to shut down commercial wildlife markets and trade, and end the import, export, and sale of live wildlife for consumption in the United States. It would also authorize funding to reduce demand for wildlife markets.

The bill’s authors, Reps. Mike Quigley (D, IL-5) and Fred Upton (R, MI6) in the House and Sens. Cory Booker (D-NJ) and John Cornyn (R-TX) in the Senate, argue that the wildlife trade poses a significant global health risk. COVID-19, Ebola, and HIV/AIDS are among a number of viruses that originated from animals.
