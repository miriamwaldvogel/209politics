H.Res. 24

Resolution - Impeaching Donald John Trump, President of the United States, for high crimes and misdemeanors

Policy Area: Congress

Introduced: 1/11/21

Sponsor: Rep. David Cicilline (D, RI-1) | view cosponsors

Committees: Judiciary

Latest action: 1/13/21 at 4:36 pm - Passed in the House 232-197.

Status:

Introduced

Passed House

View legislation text

H.Res. 24 is the article of impeachment for President Donald J. Trump, charging that he incited violence against the American government in attempting to overturn the results of the presidential election, leading to the storming of the Capitol by his supporters. The article also calls for him to be disqualified from ever holding office again.

All 222 House Democrats voted for impeachment, along with 10 Republicans, the most members of the president’s party out of all four impeachments. Trump is the first president to be impeached twice, and will face a trial in the Senate, likely after he leaves office on January 20.
